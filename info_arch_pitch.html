Crime modelling

Dr Gabriel Rosser and Prof Tao Cheng, SpacetimeLab UCL

We consider the univariate case. Consider a counting process $N(t)$ such taht $$N(t_j)-N(t_i) \sim \textrm{Pois}\left((t_j-t_i)\lambda\right)$$

This process has an associated conditional intensity: $$\lambda:=\lim_{h\to 0} \frac{\mathrm E\left(N(t,t+h)-N(t)\right)}{h}$$

This process has an associated conditional intensity: \begin{align*} \lambda(t|\mathcal{F}^-_{(-\infty,t)}) &= \mu + \eta\phi_\theta * N\\ &= \mu + \eta\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\phi_\theta(t-s)dN(s)\\ &= \mu + \eta\int_{-\infty}^{t}\phi_\theta(t-s)dN(s)\\ &= \mu + \eta\sum_{t_i\lt t}\phi_\theta(t-t_i) \end{align*}
