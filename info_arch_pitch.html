Hawkes process crash course

We consider the univariate case of a generic point process. Take a counting process $N(t)$ such that $$N(t_j)-N(t_i) \sim \textrm{Pois}\left((t_j-t_i)\lambda\right)$$

This process has an associated conditional intensity: $$\lambda:=\lim_{h\to 0} \frac{\mathrm E\left(N(t,t+h)-N(t)\right)}{h}$$

The Hawkes process in particular has an associated conditional intensity: \begin{align*} \lambda(t|\mathcal{F}^-_{(-\infty,t)}) &= \mu + \eta\phi_\theta * N\\ &= \mu + \eta\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\phi_\theta(t-s)dN(s)\\ &= \mu + \eta\int_{-\infty}^{t}\phi_\theta(t-s)dN(s)\\ &= \mu + \eta\sum_{t_i\lt t}\phi_\theta(t-t_i) \end{align*} (Hawkes, 1971)

The Maximum Likelihood estimator of this mode's parameters this has a simple form: \begin{align*} L_\theta(t_{1:N}) &:= -\int_0^T\lambda^*_\theta(t)dt + \int_0^T\log \lambda^*_\theta(t) dN_t\\ &= -\int_0^T\lambda^*_\theta(t)dt + \sum_{t_j \leq t_i} \log \lambda^*_\theta(t_j) \end{align*} $$\hat{\theta}(t_{1:N}) = \operatorname{argmax}_\theta L_\theta(t_{1:N})$$ (Ozaki, 1979.)
